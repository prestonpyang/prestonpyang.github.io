Part Brief & Ownership

Working from a provided CAD model, I programmed CNC toolpaths in Fusion 360 CAM, planned a two-sided process with a stock flip, and machined a test piece of the ball joint housing on a Tormach 770M CNC mill for the BAJA suspension assembly. I validated the process on an aluminum test piece, then applied the same workflow to produce the final steel housing.

My role: full ownership of CAM programming, machining, and process validation.

Roughing was conservative, then a single end mill dialed in the critical bores with a few light finishing passes for tighter tolerance, using one tool for both roughing and finishing.

G-code path visualization highlighting the tool paths
Machining & Problem Solving
Full machining timelapse (left) and a mid-process still (right)

I ran the full cut cycle, first side, stock flip, second side, on an aluminum test piece to de-risk the steel run and confirm setup, finish, and first-article measurements. That validation step made the steel pass predictable.

Inspection

First-article measurements on the aluminum test piece confirmed setup, finish, and hole locations across the stock flip before committing the steel stock, so the final part carried a process that was already proven rather than being validated on the final piece itself.
