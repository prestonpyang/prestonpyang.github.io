BAJA SAE Rear Upright DFM Redesign

Redesigned the right rear upright to eliminate non-functional geometry, simplifying the bore contour and standardizing all fillets and chamfers, with the bearing bore and mounting positions held to print.

Overview & Ownership

The bearing bore and hole pattern were carried over as fixed requirements: the upright locates the wheel bearing and suspension pickups, so those features remained unchanged. The prior revision included a non-functional contour around the main bore and an unsymmetric fillet at the tie-rod mount, both of which added machining time without contributing to part stiffness. The redesign was a feature-by-feature review of the part, removing geometry that did not serve a structural or functional purpose.

My role: owned the redesign end to end, from identifying non-functional geometry through the CAD revision to releasing the toleranced drawing for the machine shop.

What Changed

Bore diameter, tolerance, and hole locations are unchanged from the prior revision. The scope was limited to non-functional geometry: features simplified where possible, and fillets and chamfers standardized to reduce tooling changeovers.

  • Simplified the complex contour around the main bore down to what the bearing actually needs
  • Removed the unsymmetric fillet near the tie-rod mount and replaced it with a nominal, symmetric radius
  • Standardized every fillet on the part to nominal radii matched to the shop's available end-mill sizes, instead of one-off radii
  • Removed chamfers that weren't doing anything functionally
  • Turned the inner bore chamfer into a fillet for better tool accessibility during machining
